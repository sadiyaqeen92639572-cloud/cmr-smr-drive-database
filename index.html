CMR vs SMR: What the Difference Really Means

CMR (Conventional Magnetic Recording) writes tracks independently. Random writes are fast and consistent — 150–200 MB/s sustained regardless of workload. Drives handle RAID rebuild I/O without performance collapse.

SMR (Shingled Magnetic Recording) overlaps tracks like roof shingles to pack more data per platter. Sequential reads are fine, but any write requires rewriting an entire zone (512KB–256MB). Under sustained NAS workloads, effective write speed drops to single-digit MB/s.

WD Red: The SMR Controversy Explained

In 2020, Western Digital quietly shipped SMR drives under the WD Red branding without disclosure — drives marketed specifically for NAS. After community backlash, WD:

  • Rebranded CMR versions as WD Red Plus (EFRX/EFZZ suffix)
  • Kept WD Red name for SMR versions (EFAX suffix)
  • Created WD Red Pro as the high-RPM CMR line

Check your model suffix: EFAX = SMR, EFRX/EFZZ/EFBX = CMR.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is WD Red CMR or SMR?
WD Red (EFAX suffix models, 2020+) is SMR — problematic for NAS RAID. WD Red Plus (EFRX/EFZZ/EFBX) is CMR and safe for NAS. WD Red Pro (FFBX/KFBX/KFGX) is always CMR with 7200 RPM. Always check the model number suffix before buying.
Is Seagate IronWolf CMR?
Yes — all Seagate IronWolf and IronWolf Pro drives are CMR. Seagate never shipped SMR in the IronWolf NAS line. IronWolf runs at 5400–5900 RPM; IronWolf Pro at 7200 RPM with 300 TB/year workload rating.
Is Toshiba N300 CMR?
Yes. All Toshiba N300 drives are CMR, running at 7200 RPM with 256–512MB cache. The N300 is specifically designed for NAS use with TLER support and 24/7 operation rating. Good alternative to IronWolf at similar $/TB.
Why does SMR cause NAS problems?
SMR drives rewrite entire zones (512KB–256MB) for every random write once the drive cache is full. During RAID rebuilds — which generate sustained random writes across the entire drive — SMR speed collapses to 5–20 MB/s. A 4TB RAID rebuild that takes 6 hours on CMR can take 10+ days on SMR, causing controller timeouts and array failure.
Are WD Blue drives CMR?
Mixed. WD Blue 1TB (WD10EZEX) is CMR at 7200 RPM. WD Blue 2TB, 4TB, and 6TB (EZAZ suffix) are SMR. Desktop drives are not recommended for NAS regardless — they lack TLER firmware and NAS-specific vibration compensation.
Is Seagate Barracuda SMR?
Mixed. Barracuda 1TB (ST1000DM010) and 2TB (ST2000DM008) are CMR at 7200 RPM. 4TB, 6TB, and 8TB Barracuda drives are SMR. Not recommended for NAS regardless — desktop-class firmware without TLER.
Can I use SMR drives in a NAS if I don't use RAID?
Yes, with caveats. SMR drives work fine as single-drive NAS storage (e.g. Synology Hybrid RAID with one drive, or a simple JBOD share). The problems appear under sustained random writes: large file copies, background scrubs, or RAID rebuilds. For a media-serving NAS without redundancy, SMR is acceptable but CMR is still safer long-term.
What does TLER mean on NAS drives?
Time-Limited Error Recovery (TLER) limits how long a drive attempts to recover a read error before reporting to the RAID controller — typically 7 seconds. Desktop drives without TLER may spend minutes on a single error recovery, causing the RAID controller to mark the drive as failed. NAS-specific drives (IronWolf, Red Plus, N300) include TLER as standard.
